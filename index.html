About

As an active person and a runner, I enjoy exploring new places and keeping my days interesting. Many others seek the same thing, which is why I am sharing my personal research on running routes in Spokane. I find cool places to explore, and provide you with the details to assist you to do some exploring of your own!

BOWL AND PITCHER

4427 N Aubrey L White Pkwy, Spokane, WA

DISTANCE: 3–16 miles recommended

DIFFICULTY: Mostly flat, some rolling hills

SURFACE: Packed dirt, gravel, and concrete

ENVIRONMENT: Forested trails along the Spokane River

WILDLIFE: Deer, Bald Eagle, River Otter, Shiras Moose

MANITO PARK LOOP

1702 S Grand Blvd, Spokane, WA

DISTANCE: 3-4 miles recommended - park loops

DIFFICULTY: Rather hilly in many areas

SURFACE: Paved paths, grass, and some gravel

ENVIRONMENT: Urban park with gardens and pond

WILDLIFE: Ducks, geese, squirrels

CENTENNIAL TRAIL

Many startpoints located along the Spokane river: From Downriver Park to Cour d'Alene

DISTANCE: Up to 37 miles

DIFFICULTY: Mostly flat

SURFACE: Asphalt trail

ENVIRONMENT: Riverfront trail

WILDLIFE: Deer, Bald Eagle, Osprey

PALISADES PARK

Rimrock View Trail & Park

DISTANCE: 4–8 miles recommended: lots of areas to explore!

DIFFICULTY: Moderate hills

SURFACE: Dirt trails

ENVIRONMENT: Hillside trails overlooking Spokane

WILDLIFE: Deer, hawks, wild turkeys

FISH LAKE TRAIL

821 S Milton St

DISTANCE: 8 miles of a single track

DIFFICULTY: Flat

SURFACE: Asphalt bike path

ENVIRONMENT: Forest and lake views

WILDLIFE: Deer, beavers, waterfowl
